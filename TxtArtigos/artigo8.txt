### TITLE ###

Ratio fluorometric determination of ATP base on the reversion of fluorescence of calcein quenched by Eu(III) ion using carbon dots as reference

### ABSTRACT ###

A kind of nitrogen doped carbon dots (NCDs) with excellent stable luminescence performance was prepared by pyrolysis using ethanolamine as precursor. By simply mixing solution of NCDs and calcein-Eu3+, a ratio fluorometric probe with carbon dots as "internal reference" and calcein-Eu3+ as recognition group was constructed for ATP detection. The fluorescence of the calcein can be selectively quenched by Eu3+, and can be restored when ATP was added because Eu3+ ions exhibit higher affinity to the oxygen-donor atoms originated from phosphates than that from carboxylate groups. Meanwhile, fluorescence of NCDs was not affected by Eu3+, calcein or ATP. By adding NCDs as "internal reference" in the above system, a new ratiometric strategy for detecting ATP was conducted. The dynamic linear range for ATP detection was 5.0 × 10−8 mol L−1~ 2.0 × 10−6 mol L−1, and the detection limit was 2.0 × 10−8 mol L−1.The method was successfully applied to detecting ATP in adenosine disodium triphosphate injection. Compared with calcein- Eu3+ probe without NCDs as reference, the ratio fluorometric probe effectively reduced interference and improved the accuracy and sensitivity.

### INTRODUCTION ###

As a common energy rich phosphate compound in cells, adenosine 5′-triphosphate (ATP) provide energy directly for most of cellular events such as active transport, enzymatic processes and muscle contraction [1]. In the meanwhile, excessive wastage of ATP can be closely tied with some diseases, including cancer, ischemia and hypoglycemia [2,3]. Hence, various methods have been proposed for the determination of ATP by colorimetry [4,5], chemiluminescence [6,7], electrochemical assay [8], fluorescence [9–11]. By reason of high sensitivity and specificity, fluorescence has been being applied widely. For example, Yu et al. [10] found that the energy transfer of the ternary complex prulifloxacin-ATP-Tb3+ can more easily occurs due to effect of packing and cooperation. The fluorescence of prulifloxacin-Tb3+ increased greatly by adding ATP owing to the formation of ternary complex via electrostatic interaction and oxygen atoms in phosphate ions. Fang et al. [11] mixed carboxyfluorescein-labeled ATP aptamers and graphene oxide, causing the fluorescence quenching of carboxyfluorescein. The fluorescence of carboxyfluorescein was recovered by the addition of ATP because the stronger affinity between ATP and ATP aptamers caused desorption of the aptamers and graphene oxide.
Ratio fluorometric probes can emit two or more distinguishable wavelength at a single excitation wavelength. Using the ratio of fluorescence intensity at different wavelengths as analysis signal can effectively eliminate background interference and enhance precision and stability [12]. Several studies had been described applications of ratiometric fluorescent methods for ATP detection. Tang et al. [13] designed a rhodamine-based ratio fluorometric probe for the determination of ATP. Upon the addition of ATP, the ring of rhodamine spirolactam opened, leading to naphthalimide green emission decreased and rhodamine red emission appeared. An et al. [14] exploited a FRET probe for the detection of ATP that is based on the binding-induced modulation of fluorescence resonance energy transfer (FRET) coupled with the aggregation-caused quenching (ACQ) sensing mechanism. After adding ATP, the formation of polythiophene/ATP complex made the polythiophene skeleton from a random coil to the planar conformation and aggregation.
Carbon dots (CDs) are a kind of novel carbon nanomaterials with excellent physical and chemical properties, such as photo-stability, low toxicity, good biocompatibility and high aqueous stability [15,16]. CDs have been extensively used as bio-imaging marker, sensors and adsorbent [17–21]. Recently there are some reports about CDs-based ratio fluorometric probe [22]. Among that, CDs can act as reference signal [23,24], energy donor [25,26], response signal [27] and dual-emission matrix [28–31]. Amjadi et al. [23] conducted a ratio fluorometric sensor for sensitive and selective determination of diniconazole, in which CDs entrapped in silica core serve as the reference signal and CdTe/CdS quantum dots exert analytical signal. After adding diniconazole, fluorescence of CdTe/CdS quantum dots is selectively quenched but CDs have scarcely changes. A ratio fluorometric probe, which included CDs as sensing signal and Rhodamine B as reference, for detection of Hg2+ and glutathione has been designed by Lu et al. [27] The fluorescence of CDs was quenched by Hg2+ and recovered gradually by glutathione while fluorescence of Rhodamine B was not affected.
In this paper, the highly luminescent stable N-doped carbon dots (NCDs) were prepared by pyrolysis method only using ethanolamine as precursor. By simply mixing NCDs, calcein and Eu3+ solutions, a ratio fluorometric probe with NCDs as reference and calcein-Eu3+ as analytical signal for ATP detection was proposed. As shown in Scheme 1, solution containing NCDs and calcein exhibited two emission peaks at 428 nm and 515 nm. After adding Eu3+, the fluorescence of calcein at 515 nm was quenched effectively while that of NCDs at 428 nm had no change. Sequentially, the fluorescence of calcein-Eu3+ was regained with the addition of ATP due to the stronger interaction between ATP and Eu3+, while the fluorescence of NCDs remained invariable. Hence, using ratio of fluorescence intensity at 515 nm and 428 nm as output signal, an “on-off-on” fluorescent method for the determination of ATP was established. The ratio fluorometric probe showed low interference, high accuracy and high sensitivity compared with calcein-europium probe without NCDs as reference.


### EXPERIMENTAL ###



### INSTRUMENTATIONS ###

The fluorescence spectra were attained on a LS-55 spectrofluorometer (PerkinElmer, USA). UV–vis absorption spectra were measured on Cary 60 UV–vis spectrophotometer (Agilent Technologies, Palo Alto, CA, USA). ZEN 3690 Zeta Potentiometer (Malvern Instruments Co., Ltd, Malvern, UK) and Tecnai G2 20 Transmission Electron Microscope (FEI Company, Eindhoven, The Netherlands) was applied for characterization of NCDs. TG16-WS high speed centrifuge (Changsha Xiangzhi Centrifuge Instrument Co., Ltd., Changsha, China) was used for separation of NCDs. Buffer solution was adjusted by pHS-3C Thunder Magnetic Acidity Meter (Shanghai Precision Scientific Instrument Co., Ltd., Shanghai, China).


### MATERIALS AND REAGENTS ###

All chemicals were of analytical reagent grade. Ethanolamine, Nitric acid, Calcein, sodium hydroxide, hydrogen peroxide (H2O2, 30%) and H2SO4 (98%) were supplied from Sinopharm Chemical Reagent Co., Ltd. (Shanghai, China). Adenosine triphosphate were purchased from Wuhan Huashun Biotechnology Co., Ltd. (Wuhan, China), Trishydroxymethylaminomethane was obtained from Shanghai Huajie Fine Chemical Co., Ltd. (Shanghai, China). Doubly deionized water was used throughout.


### ONE-STEP SYNTHESIS OF N-DOPED CARBON QUANTUM DOTS ###

N-doped CDs were prepared by pyrolyzing Ethanolamine described in the literature [32]. Briefly, 3.0 mL Ethanolamine and 4.5 mL H2O2 were added into a 200 mL beaker and mixed homogeneously. Then, the mixture was heated at 180 °C for 30 min in an oven to brown viscous liquid. The product was added into 100 mL H2O. Finally, the N-doped CDs were collected via centrifugation at 8000 rpm for 30 min.


### ATP DETECTION IN A REAL SAMPLE ###

200 μL 1.35 mg mL−1 NCDs solution, 200 μL 1.0 × 10−4 mol L−1 Calcein solution, 1 mL Tris-HCl buffer(pH=7.3) and 200 μL 6.0 × 10−5 mol L−1 Eu3+ solution were transferred into a colorimetric tube. Then, 20 μL of standard solution or treated sample solution containing ATP was added into the tube and finally diluted to 10 mL with distilled water.
The fluorescence intensity was measured at excitation wavelength of 340 nm, and emission wavelengths of 428 nm (NCDs) and 515 nm (Calcein). The excitation and emission slits was set as 10 nm and 4.5 nm. The speed of scan was 400 nm min−1. Each sample was measured in parallel three times.


### RESULTS AND DISCUSSION ###



### CHARACTERIZATION OF NCDS ###

NCDs were synthesized by a one-step thermal decomposition method using Ethanolamine as the precursor at 180 °C for 30 min. As shown in Fig. 1A, the as-prepared NCDs exhibited a spherical or spheroidal morphology and were equally distributed, relatively. Infrared spectrum (IR) implied that the existence of functional groups on the surface of the NCDs, mainly -OH, -NH2 -CONH, which enabled the NCDs good water solubility (see in the Supplementary material, Fig. S1). X-ray photoelectron spectroscopy analysis data (Fig. S2) consistent with results described by FT-IR spectrum, illustrated that N was efficiently doped in CDs, and -OH, -NH2, -CONH groups are on the surface of NCDs. The NCDs solution stored at 4 °C for several months remained stable without deposition.
The optical properties of NCDs were Characterized using fluorescence spectrum and UV–vis spectroscopy. As indicated in Fig. 1C, NCDs solution possessed a strong absorption peak and can emit bright blue fluorescence exposed to UV lamp (365 nm) but exhibit weak yellow in the sunlight. The fluorescent intensity of NCDs was first increased and then decreased when the excitation wavelength changed from 320 nm to 460 nm (Fig. 1D). That indicated different emission centers were existed because of several groups on the surface of CDs and the dominant emission centers will alternate as the excitation wavelength changed [33]. Meanwhile, the maximum fluorescence peak was at 360 nm of excitation wavelength and the emission peak is at 440 nm.


### LUMINESCENCE STABILITY OF NCDS ###

We explored effects of external environment on the fluorescence intensity of NCDs, such as pH value, mental ions, time of store and exposure time under UV light. As shown in Fig. S3A, the FL intensity of NCDs changed a lot under strong acid and alkali conditions. But at pH range from 4 to 9, the fluorescence intensity of NCDs was basically constant. Except for the weak quenching of NCDs by Cu2+, Hg2+ and Fe3+, the presence of other metal ions did not affect the fluorescence of NCDs (Fig. S3B). As seen in Fig. S3C, the fluorescence intensity of NCDs stored for 12 months were only decreased a bit. Also, it possessed good photo-bleaching resistance under continuous illumination with UV light (λ = 365 nm), which was shown in Fig. S3D. The fluorescence intensity was decayed slightly with exposure time increased to 3 h, which remained more than 90% of initial intensity. These suggested that the as-prepared NCDs have excellent stability.


### SPECTRAL PROPERTIES OF NCDS AND CALCEIN ###

Fig. S4 shows the UV–vis spectra and fluorescence spectra of NCDs and calcein. As shown in Fig. S4A, the absorption peak of calcein remained almost constant after adding NCDs，and the absorption spectrum of mixture (curve c) were similar to curve d (sum of curve a and curve c). Fluorescence spectra (Fig. S4B) showed that the fluorescence of NCDs was stable when mixed with calcein. But the fluorescence of calcein decreased apparently for internal filtering. These also suggested that the NCDs have excellent stability, which confirmed that the fluorescence of NCDs are stable enough and can be used as “internal reference” in the following study.


### EFFECTS OF EU3+ AND ATP ON THE FLUORESCENCE OF CALCEIN AND NCDS ###

Firstly, the effects of Eu3+ and ATP on the fluorescence of calcein were studied. As depicted in Fig. 2A, calcein possessed a strong fluorescence peak at 525 nm(curve a). After Eu3+ added, the fluorescence of calcein quenched due to the interaction between calcein and Eu3+ (curve b). Continually, the fluorescence of calcein can be obviously recovered by ATP for its strong coordination ability towards Eu3+(curve c). According to the fluorescence recovery of calcein, a method for ATP can be established.
To verify the feasibility of NCDs as “internal standard” in the above system for ATP detection, the fluorescence of NCDs-Calcein-Eu3+ in the absence and presence of ATP was investigated. As shown in Fig. 2B, mixture of NCDs-Calcein had two emission peak at 428 nm and 515 nm (curve a). After Eu3+ was added, the fluorescence of calcein at 428 nm was markedly quenched whereas the fluorescence of NCDs at 515 nm was almost constant (curve b). With ATP added, the fluorescence of calcein recovered whereas the NCDs’ still remained the same (curve c). Therefore, by using fluorescence ratio of 515 nm to 428 nm (F515/F428) as signal, a fluoremetric method can be constructed for the determination of ATP.


### OPTIMIZATION OF CONDITIONS FOR ATP DETERMINATION ###



### EFFECT OF PH ###

The Effect of pH on fluorescence of NCDs-Calcein-Eu3+ in the absence and presence of ATP was investigated, and the results are shown in Fig. S5. As shown in Fig. S5, fluorescence ratio of 515 nm to 428 nm(F515/F428) of NCDs-calcein-Eu3+ increased with the increasing of pH (curve a). These might be due to the hydrolysis of Eu3+ in alkaline environment and prohibiting of fluorescence quenching [34]. The fluorescence of NCDs-Calcein only can be effectively quenched by Eu3+ within pH 7.1–7.6. The Effect of pH on fluorescence of NCDs-Calcein-Eu3+ in presence of ATP (Fig. S5 curve b) showed that the fluorescence recovered apparently while pH within 7.1–7.6, and decreased with the increasing of pH. These might because the interaction between Eu3+ and ATP also was restricted in alkaline condition. Therefore, buffer solution of pH 7.3 was chosen for the detection of ATP.
Influence of Eu3+ concentration
The quenching fluorescence of calcein by different Eu3+ concentration was also studied ( Fig. 3). The FL intensity of calcein at 515 nm decreased with the increasing of Eu3+ concentration and not decreased while the concentration of Eu3+ higher than 1.2 × 10−6 mol L−1, suggesting that a stable complex had formed between calcein and Eu3+. So 1.2 × 10−6 mol L−1 was chosen as the best Eu3+ concentration for the following study.


### INFLUENCE OF REACTION TIME ###

As shown in Fig. S6, the fluorescence of calcein can reached a maximum value in 10 min and kept almost constant more than 40 min. So, 15 min was chosen as the optimal reaction time for the system.


### ANALYTICAL APPLICATION ###



### PERFORMANCE OF RATIO FLUOROMETRIC PROBES AND COMPARISON ###

Under the optimized condition, the analytical performance of the probe with and without NCDs was carefully studied. As depicted in Fig. 4, the fluorescence intensity of calcein at 515 nm increased gradually with ATP added whereas the NCDs at 428 nm was nearly constant (Fig. 4A). A good linear relationship between F515 / F428 and the concentration of ATP was obtained in the range of 5.0 × 10−8 mol L−1 to 2.0 × 10 −6 mol L−1. The equation was F515/F428 = 0.609 + 0.252CATP (C: ×10−6 mol L−1，R2 = 0.9988，n = 10). The detection limit was calculated to be 2.0 × 10−8 mol L−1 (Fig. 4B) and the relative standard deviation (R.S.D.) was 0.33% for determination of 5.0 × 10−8 mol L−1 ATP. Simultaneously, the probe without NCDs as reference was explored (Fig. 4C and D). The result suggested that the linear range was only 1.0 × 10−7 to 2.0 × 10−6 mol L−1, and the correlation coefficient of calibration curve was 0.96, which was much lower than ratio fluorometric methods using NCDs as reference. These confirmed that the proposed ratio fluorometric method can eliminate environmental influence to make it wider linear range, higher sensitive and higher precision.


### ATP DETERMINATION FOR REAL SAMPLE ###

To verify the feasibility, the ratio fluorometric probe (NCDs-calcein- Eu3+) was used for detection of ATP in adenosine disodium triphosphate injection. As shown in Table 1, the concentration of ATP in the diluted sample was 3.39 × 10−7 mol L−1 (RSD = 6.9%, n = 3), and the calculated concentration of original sample was 10.25 mg mL−1, which was the same with the labeling value (10 mg mL−1). The recoveries for detection were between 96% and 109% and the relative standard deviations were below 3%, which indicated the applicability of ATP determination in real sample.


### CONCLUSIONS ###

In summary, the NCDs with stable emission properties were prepared successfully by one-step pyrolysis using ethanolamine used as carbon source and nitrogen source. A ratio fluorometric probe for ATP detection was established by mixing NCDs and calcein-Eu3+. The fluorescence of calcein quenched by Eu3+ can recovered while ATP added, whereas fluorescence of NCDs not changed. Compared with calcein-Eu3+ probe without NCDs as reference, the ratio fluorometric probe can reduce interference availably and improve accuracy and sensitivity. This kind of NCDs with stable emission properties might be widely applied to various ratio fluorometric methods by using fluorescence of NCDs as “internal reference”.
